Morocco Completes Stadium Upgrades for AFCON 2025

May 15, 2024 By CAF Media 24 Comments
Mohammed V Stadium in Casablanca after renovations

The Moroccan government has announced the completion of major renovations to six stadiums across the country in preparation for the Africa Cup of Nations 2025. The upgrades include state-of-the-art facilities and improved accessibility features that will enhance the experience for both players and spectators.

Minister of Sports, Chakib Benmoussa, confirmed that all stadiums are now ready to host matches after undergoing extensive modernization. "We have worked tirelessly to ensure our facilities meet the highest international standards," Benmoussa stated during a press conference in Rabat.

Key Upgrades Include:

  • New seating with improved sightlines in all venues
  • Upgraded locker rooms and player facilities
  • Enhanced media centers with modern broadcasting capabilities
  • Improved accessibility for disabled fans
  • State-of-the-art security systems

The Grand Stade de Marrakech, which will host the tournament final, has received particular attention with the installation of a new hybrid pitch and expanded VIP areas. Other venues undergoing significant upgrades include the Mohammed V Stadium in Casablanca and the Stade Ibn Battouta in Tangier.

"These world-class facilities demonstrate Morocco's commitment to hosting an exceptional tournament that will showcase the best of African football," said CAF President Patrice Motsepe during an inspection tour.

With the stadiums now complete, organizers can focus on final preparations for the tournament, which kicks off on January 13, 2025. Ticket sales are expected to begin next month, with high demand anticipated from across the continent.
